ajf's NHS Scotland transgender/gender dysphoria treatment resources

Since this is an area where not much information seems to be out there, or certainly, not much that is easy to find, I'm gathering information about NHS Scotland transgender/gender dysphoria treatment here.

  • Documents of note, references:
    1. Scottish Gender Reassignment Protocol
    2. Sandyford Gender Identity Services Information booklet
    3. change.org petition - NHS Grampian: Change transgender care in Grampian to truly reflect their needs.
  • NHS Scotland "Gender clinics", there are four of these:[1]
    • Sandyford (Glasgow)
      • The largest in Scotland and the main clinic.
      • Accepts referrals from across Scotland and also self-referrals allowed.
      • Has a good booklet about its services: [2] (contains details on treatment and how to self-refer)
      • By the sounds of things, the clinic is quite good.
    • Sexual Problems Clinic within Royal Infirmary of Edinburgh.
      • Accepts patients from NHS Lothian Health Board area and also NHS Fife, NHS Borders, NHS Forth Valley, NHS Tayside and the North of England.
      • Can be contacted on 0131 242 2515.
    • NHS Grampian
      • Referrals are accepted from general practitioners of patients residing in Grampian, Orkney and Shetland.
      • Referrals should be made to Dr John Callender, Royal Cornhill Hospital, Aberdeen AB25 7ZH.
      • Some bad things about said it on this petition: [3] - there is little information about Dr. Callender other than that which I can find, please email me if you have any.
    • NHS Highland Sexual Health Clinic based at Raigmore Hospital, Inverness
      • Accepts self referrals.
      • The clinic does not accept out of area referrals.
      • Can be contacted on 01463 704202.
  • Under 18s
    • "Teenagers who are 16 and 17 years of age are entitled to consent to their own treatment and follow the standard adult protocol, and this consent cannot be overruled by their parents. Children who are under 16 years old can consent to their own treatment if it is thought that they have enough intelligence, competence and understanding to fully appreciate what is involved in their treatment."[1]
    • "At present specialist gender identity development services for children and young people under 16 are not available in Scotland. Children and young people should contact their GP or a GIC in the first instance and thereafter may be referred to the Gender Identity Development Service at The Tavistock and Portman NHS Foundation Trust, London."[1]
