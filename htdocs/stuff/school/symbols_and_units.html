Symbols and Units

Quantity (symbol) Unit (symbol) Example
Distance (d) Meter (m) v = d/t
4m/s = 4m/1s
Time (t) Second (s)
Speed (v, or sometimes u where v is final speed and u is initial speed) Meters per second (m/s or ms-1)
Acceleration (a) Meters per second per second (m/s/s or m/s2) a = (v-u)/t
10m/s2 = (20m/s - 10m/s)/1s
Wavelength (λ) Meters (m) v = fλ
4m/s = 2Hz * 2m
Frequency (f) Hertz (Hz)
Voltage (V) Volt (V) P = IV
10W = 2A * 5V
Current (I) Amp (A)
Power (P) Watt (W)
Resistance (R) Ohm (Ω) V = IR
4V = 1A * 4Ω
Energy (E) Joule (J) E = Pt
100J = 100W * 1s
Charge (Q) Coulomb (C) Q = It
10C = 2A * 4s
Force (F) Newton (N) F = ma
10N = 5kg * 2m/s2
Mass (m) Kilogram (kg)
Gravitational field strength (g) Newtons per Kilogram (N/kg) Ep = mgh
30kg = 1kg * 10N/kg * 3m
Weight (W) Newton (N) W = mg
20N = 2kg * 10N/kg
Heat capacity (may be represented by c, for constant) Joules per kilogram per degree Celsius (J/kg°C) Eh = cm∆T
60J = 10J/kg°C * 1kg * 60°C
Latent heat (L) Joules per kilogram (J/kg) Eh = mL
60J = 6kg * 10J/kg
